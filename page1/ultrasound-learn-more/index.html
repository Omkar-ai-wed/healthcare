üî¨ How Ultrasound Technology Works

Ultrasound imaging uses high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images of internal body structures. This completely safe, non-invasive technology has revolutionized medical imaging by providing immediate diagnostic information without radiation exposure.

1

Sound Wave Transmission

Transducer sends high-frequency sound waves (2-18 MHz) into the body

Ultrasound uses sound waves far above human hearing range, completely safe and non-invasive
üì°
2

Echo Reflection

Sound waves bounce back from different tissues at varying speeds

Different tissues reflect sound differently, creating unique echo patterns for each organ
üîÑ
3

Signal Processing

Computer analyzes returning echoes and their timing to create images

Advanced computer processing converts echo data into real-time visual images
üíª
4

Real-time Display

Creates live, moving images displayed on screen instantly

Unlike other imaging, ultrasound shows organs and blood flow in real-time motion

üîç Types of Ultrasound Examinations

Transabdominal Ultrasound

Very comfortable
Description: External probe placed on skin surface with gel
Uses: Pregnancy, abdominal organs, heart, kidneys
Preparation: May require full bladder for pelvic exams
Duration: 15-45 minutes

Transvaginal Ultrasound

Well-tolerated
Description: Internal probe inserted into vagina for detailed pelvic imaging
Uses: Early pregnancy, gynecologic evaluation, pelvic pain
Preparation: Empty bladder, comfortable positioning
Duration: 15-30 minutes

Transrectal Ultrasound

Generally well-tolerated
Description: Specialized probe for detailed prostate evaluation
Uses: Prostate assessment, biopsy guidance
Preparation: Enema may be required, positioned on side
Duration: 15-30 minutes

Doppler Ultrasound

No additional discomfort
Description: Measures blood flow speed and direction using sound wave frequency changes
Uses: Vascular assessment, heart valve function, blood clots
Preparation: Same as standard ultrasound
Duration: 20-45 minutes

3D/4D Ultrasound

Same comfort level
Description: Three-dimensional imaging with 4D adding real-time motion
Uses: Detailed fetal imaging, complex anatomy evaluation
Preparation: Similar to standard ultrasound
Duration: 30-60 minutes
