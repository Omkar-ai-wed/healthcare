How These Technologies Work

X-rays pass through your body, being absorbed differently by various tissues. Dense materials like bones appear white, while soft tissues appear in shades of gray, and air appears black.

CT scanners rotate around your body, taking multiple X-ray images from different angles. Computer algorithms reconstruct these into detailed cross-sectional images.

Both technologies follow ALARA principles (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) to minimize radiation exposure while maintaining diagnostic quality.

Contrast Dye Safety Information

Types of Contrast

  • Iodine-based (IV contrast)
  • Barium sulfate (oral contrast)
  • Gadolinium (rare for CT)

Possible Side Effects

  • Warm sensation (normal)
  • Metallic taste (normal)
  • Nausea (rare)
  • Allergic reaction (very rare)

Preparation Required

Fast 4-6 hours before contrast studies. Stay hydrated after the procedure to help eliminate contrast from your system.

X-Ray Technology

  • Energy Range: 30-150 kV
  • Resolution: 0.1-0.2 mm detail
  • Digital advantages: Immediate viewing
  • Equipment: DR/CR systems

CT Technology

  • Slice Thickness: 0.5-10 mm
  • Image Matrix: 512x512 to 1024x1024
  • Scan Speed: 0.3-2 seconds per rotation
  • Detectors: 16 to 320+ detector rows

Frequently Asked Questions

The radiation doses used are carefully controlled and considered safe for diagnostic purposes. The medical benefits far outweigh the minimal risks. Modern equipment uses the lowest possible doses while maintaining image quality.

Always inform your doctor if you're pregnant or might be pregnant. X-rays and CT scans are generally avoided during pregnancy unless absolutely necessary for diagnosis. Alternative imaging like ultrasound may be recommended.

Preparation varies by exam type. For basic X-rays, no preparation is needed. For CT scans, you may need to fast and drink contrast solution. Your healthcare team will provide specific instructions.

X-ray results are often available immediately for emergency situations. CT scan results typically take 24-48 hours as they require detailed review by a radiologist. Your doctor will discuss results and next steps.

CT scanners have an open design and are much less confining than MRI machines. You can communicate with the technologist throughout the procedure. Mild sedation may be available if needed.
