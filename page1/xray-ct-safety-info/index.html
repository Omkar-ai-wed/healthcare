ALARA Principle

Every radiation exposure should be justified and optimized to minimize patient dose while maintaining diagnostic quality.

Contrast Agent Safety

Types of contrast agents, side effects, and reaction management

Iodinated Contrast (IV)

Blood vessels & organs

Common Side Effects:

  • Warm sensation
  • Metallic taste
  • Mild nausea

Allergic Reaction Rates:

Mild reactions: 0.2-1% | Severe: 0.02-0.04%

Barium Sulfate (Oral)

Digestive system

Common Side Effects:

  • Constipation
  • Chalky taste

Allergic Reaction Rates:

Very rare allergic reactions

Kidney Safety Considerations

Risk Factors

  • Age over 70
  • Diabetes
  • Heart failure
  • Dehydration

Prevention Measures

  • Hydration before and after
  • Hold nephrotoxic medications
  • Monitor kidney function

Contraindications

  • GFR less than 30
  • Acute kidney injury
  • Multiple myeloma

Immediate action guidelines for adverse reactions

üÜò Call 911 Immediately

Severe Difficulty Breathing

Shortness of breath, wheezing, gasping

Treatment: Oxygen, bronchodilators, epinephrine

Widespread Hives with Swelling

Facial, throat, or tongue swelling

Treatment: Antihistamines, steroids, epinephrine

Loss of Consciousness

Fainting, unresponsiveness, severe drop in blood pressure

Treatment: IV fluids, vasopressors, CPR if needed

Special Populations

Safety considerations for specific patient groups

Special Considerations

  • Higher radiation sensitivity
  • Longer life expectancy increases risk
  • Need for sedation may increase
  • Pediatric protocols reduce dose

Protection Measures

  • Lead shielding of reproductive organs
  • Immobilization devices to prevent repeats
  • Parent/caregiver presence when possible
  • Child life specialists for comfort

Special Considerations

  • Increased kidney disease prevalence
  • Multiple medications and interactions
  • Higher dehydration risk
  • Cognitive impairment affecting cooperation

Precautions

  • Careful medication review
  • Hydration assessment and support
  • Family involvement in consent process
  • Fall prevention during positioning

Diabetes

  • Monitor blood glucose levels
  • Kidney function assessment
  • Metformin hold protocols
  • Contrast-induced nephropathy risk

Heart Disease

  • Monitor cardiac rhythm
  • Blood pressure management
  • Medication interactions
  • Fluid status assessment

Asthma

  • Increased contrast reaction risk
  • Bronchodilator availability
  • Premedication consideration
  • Emergency equipment ready
