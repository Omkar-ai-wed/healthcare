Dosha Fundamentals

What Are Doshas?

Doshas are the three biological energies that govern all physiological and psychological processes in the body and mind, derived from the five elements and representing different combinations working together.

Key Constitutional Concepts

Prakriti (Birth Constitution)

Your birth constitution - the unique proportion of doshas you were born with

  • Determined at conception by parental doshas and environmental factors
  • Remains constant throughout life as your genetic blueprint
  • Represents your optimal state of health and natural tendencies
  • Influences physical appearance, mental traits, and behavioral patterns

Vikriti (Current State)

Your current constitutional state - how your doshas are functioning right now

  • Changes based on diet, lifestyle, stress, age, and environmental factors
  • May deviate significantly from birth constitution due to imbalancing factors
  • Reflects current symptoms, health challenges, and functional patterns
  • Goal of Ayurvedic treatment is to restore Vikriti closer to Prakriti

The Three Doshas

üí®

Vata Dosha

Air + Space Elements

The energy of movement and communication, governing all transportation and nervous system functions in the body

Physical Characteristics

  • Thin, light frame with prominent bones and joints
  • Very tall or very short with irregular proportions
  • Naturally underweight, difficulty gaining weight
  • Dry, rough, cool skin prone to wrinkles
  • Dry, frizzy, coarse hair prone to split ends
  • Small, dark, active eyes with darting movements
  • High-pitched voice, talks fast with animated speech
  • Very flexible, hypermobile joints that crack frequently
  • Poor circulation, cold hands and feet

Mental & Emotional Traits

  • Quick to grasp new concepts but quick to forget
  • Good short-term memory but poor long-term retention
  • Indecisive, changes mind frequently
  • Talkative, animated, jumps between topics
  • Highly creative, artistic, imaginative
  • Enthusiastic when balanced, anxious when stressed
  • Makes friends easily, enjoys variety in relationships
  • Works in creative bursts, easily distracted

Physiological Functions

  • All types of movement in the body
  • Nervous system function and nerve transmission
  • Respiratory functions including breathing
  • Circulatory system and heart rhythm
  • Elimination processes
  • Sensory perception and processing

Balanced Signs

  • Creative expression flows naturally
  • Mental alertness with quick comprehension
  • Regular elimination with healthy bowel movements
  • Enthusiasm and joy in daily activities
  • Flexibility and adaptability to change
  • Good coordination and graceful movement

Imbalanced Signs

  • Anxiety, worry, racing thoughts
  • Insomnia, difficulty falling asleep
  • Constipation, gas, bloating
  • Dry skin, joint pain, muscle stiffness
  • Difficulty concentrating, scattered mind
  • Fear, nervousness, feeling overwhelmed

Balancing Guidelines

  • Favor warm, moist, grounding foods that provide stability
  • Include sweet, sour, and salty tastes to calm Vata energy
  • Eat regular meals at consistent times to create routine
  • Choose cooked foods over raw, use healthy oils and ghee
  • Avoid cold, dry, light foods that increase Vata qualities
  • Stay well-hydrated with warm beverages throughout the day
  • Maintain regular daily routine and consistent sleep schedule
  • Create warm, comfortable environments free from excessive wind
  • Practice gentle, grounding exercises like yoga and walking
  • Regular oil massage (Abhyanga) to nourish and calm the nervous system
  • Meditation and breathing practices to calm the mind
  • Adequate rest and avoid overexertion or overstimulation
  • Ashwagandha for nervous system support and stress adaptation
  • Brahmi for mental clarity and cognitive enhancement
  • Triphala for gentle digestive support and regular elimination
  • Sesame oil for external massage and joint lubrication
  • Warm spices like ginger, cinnamon, and cardamom for digestion

Pitta Dosha

Fire + Water Elements

The energy of transformation and metabolism, governing all chemical and metabolic processes in the body

Physical Characteristics

  • Medium build, well-proportioned, naturally athletic
  • Average to tall height with balanced frame
  • Moderate weight, gains and loses weight easily when balanced
  • Soft, warm, fair complexion, sensitive to sun
  • Fine, soft, straight hair, tendency toward early graying
  • Medium size eyes with penetrating gaze, often light colored
  • Sharp, clear, articulate speech, penetrating tone
  • Medium flexibility with normal range of motion
  • Good circulation, warm body temperature, sweats easily

Mental & Emotional Traits

  • Sharp, focused learner with excellent retention
  • Excellent recall and attention to detail
  • Quick, decisive, confident in choices
  • Articulate, persuasive, direct communication
  • Practical creativity, excellent at organizing
  • Passionate when balanced, irritable when stressed
  • Selective in relationships, enjoys intellectual discussions
  • Focused, goal-oriented, natural leader

Physiological Functions

  • Digestive fire (Agni) and metabolic transformations
  • Body temperature regulation and heat production
  • Vision and eye function, light perception
  • Hormone production and endocrine balance
  • Skin health, complexion, and natural radiance
  • Blood quality and circulation efficiency

Balanced Signs

  • Sharp intellect with strong comprehension
  • Efficient digestion with robust appetite
  • Natural leadership qualities and confidence
  • Healthy competitive spirit and achievement motivation
  • Radiant complexion and strong immune system
  • Good focus, concentration, and goal-oriented behavior

Imbalanced Signs

  • Anger, irritability, impatience, critical judgment
  • Acid reflux, heartburn, ulcers, inflammatory digestive issues
  • Skin rashes, acne, inflammation, heat-related skin problems
  • Excessive sweating, overheating, heat intolerance
  • Perfectionism, workaholism, burnout from overachievement
  • Premature graying, hair loss, early aging signs

Balancing Guidelines

  • Favor cool, refreshing foods that pacify internal heat
  • Include sweet, bitter, and astringent tastes to cool Pitta
  • Eat moderate portions at regular meal times without skipping
  • Use cooling spices like coriander, fennel, and fresh mint
  • Avoid spicy, sour, salty, and fried foods that increase heat
  • Stay well-hydrated with cool (not ice-cold) beverages
  • Maintain cool environments and avoid excessive heat exposure
  • Practice moderate, non-competitive exercise like swimming
  • Avoid overheating through clothing choices and activity timing
  • Regular relaxation and stress management to prevent burnout
  • Moon salutations and cooling yoga practices
  • Time in nature, especially near water or in shaded areas
  • Aloe vera for cooling and digestive support
  • Neem for blood purification and skin health
  • Coriander for digestive cooling and inflammation reduction
  • Coconut oil for cooling external massage and skin care
  • Cooling herbs like mint, rose, and fennel for balance

Kapha Dosha

Earth + Water Elements

The energy of structure and stability, providing cohesion, immunity, and physical strength to the body

Physical Characteristics

  • Large frame, solid build, naturally strong and heavy-set
  • Average to short height with stocky, robust appearance
  • Gains weight easily, difficulty losing weight, slow metabolism
  • Thick, soft, smooth, cool, pale complexion
  • Thick, wavy, lustrous, oily, strong and dark colored hair
  • Large, beautiful eyes with calm expression, thick eyelashes
  • Deep, slow, melodious, soft and pleasant voice tone
  • Less flexible joints that feel tight and stable
  • Slow circulation, cool body temperature, tolerates cold well

Mental & Emotional Traits

  • Slow to learn but methodical, excellent long-term retention
  • Once information is learned, it is never forgotten
  • Takes time to make decisions but very firm once decided
  • Slow, thoughtful speech, good listener, diplomatic approach
  • Patient craftsmanship, traditional arts, nurturing creativity
  • Calm, loving, compassionate when balanced
  • Few close friends, loyal and supportive, family-oriented
  • Steady, methodical approach, dislikes pressure

Physiological Functions

  • Body structure including bones, muscles, connective tissue
  • Immune system function and disease resistance
  • Lubrication of joints and mucous membrane health
  • Growth processes and tissue repair mechanisms
  • Physical and emotional stability and grounding
  • Fluid balance and lymphatic system function

Balanced Signs

  • Strong immunity with rare illness and quick recovery
  • Loving, compassionate, supportive nature toward others
  • Steady energy and excellent physical endurance
  • Excellent long-term memory and methodical learning
  • Natural physical strength and emotional stability
  • Patient, methodical approach to tasks and relationships

Imbalanced Signs

  • Weight gain, sluggish metabolism, difficulty losing weight
  • Congestion, excessive mucus production, respiratory issues
  • Mental fog, depression, lethargy, lack of motivation
  • Attachment, possessiveness, greed, resistance to change
  • Oversleeping, difficulty waking up, excessive daytime sleepiness
  • Water retention, swelling, lymphatic stagnation

Balancing Guidelines

  • Favor light, warm, spicy foods that stimulate metabolism
  • Include pungent, bitter, and astringent tastes to reduce Kapha
  • Eat smaller portions and avoid overeating or heavy meals
  • Use warming spices like ginger, black pepper, and turmeric
  • Avoid heavy, cold, oily foods and excessive dairy products
  • Reduce overall food intake and consider intermittent fasting
  • Regular vigorous exercise to stimulate circulation and metabolism
  • Early rising and avoid daytime naps that increase sluggishness
  • Seek stimulating activities and social interaction for motivation
  • Create variety in daily routine to prevent stagnation
  • Dry heat therapy and warm, dry environments
  • Mental stimulation through learning and challenging activities
  • Turmeric for anti-inflammatory action and circulation improvement
  • Ginger for digestive fire stimulation and metabolism boost
  • Guggul for weight management and cholesterol regulation
  • Mustard oil for warming external massage and circulation
  • Stimulating herbs like cayenne, cinnamon, and cloves for balance

Constitutional Types & Combinations

Single Dosha Dominance

Pure constitutional types with 60% or more dominance of one dosha

Relatively uncommon, representing about 10-15% of population

Pure Vata

Characteristics: 60%+ Vata qualities with minimal Pitta and Kapha influence

Traits: Highly creative, changeable, sensitive to environment, prone to anxiety

Advantages: Exceptional creativity, adaptability, spiritual sensitivity
Challenges: Difficulty with routine, prone to imbalance, sensitive constitution

Pure Pitta

Characteristics: 60%+ Pitta qualities with minimal Vata and Kapha influence

Traits: Intense, focused, competitive, natural leaders, strong digestion

Advantages: Excellent leadership, sharp intellect, efficient metabolism
Challenges: Prone to anger, perfectionism, heat-related disorders

Pure Kapha

Characteristics: 60%+ Kapha qualities with minimal Vata and Pitta influence

Traits: Stable, calm, strong, excellent endurance, loving nature

Advantages: Strong immunity, emotional stability, natural strength
Challenges: Weight gain, sluggishness, resistance to change

Dual Dosha Combinations

Most common constitutional pattern with two doshas in significant proportions

Represents about 80-85% of population with various combinations

Vata-Pitta

Characteristics: Vata and Pitta dominance with minimal Kapha

Traits: Creative leadership, variable digestion, intense but changeable

Advantages: Creative intelligence, adaptable leadership, innovative thinking
Challenges: Burnout potential, digestive irregularity, overstimulation

Pitta-Kapha

Characteristics: Pitta and Kapha dominance with minimal Vata

Traits: Strong metabolism with physical stability, steady energy

Advantages: Strong constitution, reliable performance, good endurance
Challenges: Weight management, perfectionist tendencies, slow adaptation

Vata-Kapha

Characteristics: Vata and Kapha dominance with minimal Pitta

Traits: Creative strength, variable energy, gentle nature

Advantages: Creative endurance, compassionate nature, spiritual inclination
Challenges: Irregular energy, digestive weakness, procrastination

Tri-Doshic Constitution

Equal proportions of all three doshas in balanced combination

Very rare, representing less than 5% of population

Tri-Doshic (33-33-33%)

Characteristics: 33-33-33% distribution with natural balance and adaptability

Advantages:
  • Natural balance and equilibrium in most situations
  • Adaptability to various climates, foods, and lifestyles
  • Generally robust health and resistance to imbalances
  • Ability to thrive in diverse environments and conditions
Challenges:
  • Difficulty determining specific balancing strategies
  • May experience multiple types of imbalances simultaneously
  • Requires more individualized approach to treatment
  • Can be confused about constitutional identity and needs

Seasonal Adjustments (Ritucharya)

Spring

Dominant Dosha: Kapha season requiring energizing practices

Recommendations: Vigorous exercise, light foods, cleansing practices
Avoid: Heavy foods, oversleeping, sedentary lifestyle

Summer

Dominant Dosha: Pitta season requiring cooling practices

Recommendations: Cooling foods, moderate exercise, relaxation
Avoid: Excessive heat, spicy foods, intense competition

Fall

Dominant Dosha: Vata season requiring grounding practices

Recommendations: Regular routine, warming foods, oil massage
Avoid: Irregular schedule, cold foods, excessive travel

Winter

Dominant Dosha: Continued Vata season with some Kapha qualities

Recommendations: Warming practices, nourishing foods, adequate rest
Avoid: Cold exposure, light foods, overexertion

Exercise Recommendations by Dosha

Vata Exercise

  • Gentle, flowing movements like yoga and tai chi
  • Walking in nature, especially in warm weather
  • Swimming in warm water for joint support
  • Dance and creative movement expression
  • Avoid excessive or high-impact exercise

Pitta Exercise

  • Swimming and water sports for cooling effect
  • Cycling and moderate-intensity activities
  • Winter sports and cool-weather activities
  • Team sports with friendly competition
  • Avoid exercising in hot weather or intense heat

Kapha Exercise

  • Vigorous, energizing activities like running and aerobics
  • Weight training and strength-building exercises
  • Competitive sports for motivation and stimulation
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Morning exercise to energize for the day
